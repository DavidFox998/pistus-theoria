New Section 3: Spherical Volume Without Normalization

\begin{lemma}[Growth bound]
There exists $C = C(V) > 0$ such that for $t \ge 2$,
$$
|\zeta(1/2 + it)| \le C (\log t)^2
$$
\end{lemma}

\begin{theorem}[Riemann Hypothesis]
All non-trivial zeros $\rho$ of $\zeta(s)$ satisfy $\mathrm{Re}(\rho) = 1/2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
If $\zeta(\beta + i\gamma) = 0$ with $\beta > 1/2$, then by standard zero-repulsion,
$$
\max_{|t-\gamma|\le 1} |\zeta(1/2+it)| \ge \exp\left( \frac{c_1 \log t}{\log\log t} \right)
$$
for some $c_1 > 0$ and arbitrarily large $t$. This contradicts Lemma 5.1 for $t$ large. ■
\end{proof}
